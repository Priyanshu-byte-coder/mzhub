We all have them: boxes of old cassette tapes, stacks of DVDs, or hard drives full of digital files gathering dust. For faith institutions, cultural organizations, and public broadcasters, these aren't just "files." **They are sermons that moved hearts, oral histories from elders who are no longer with us, and chants that define a community.**

These recordings are some of the most valuable sources of spiritual and cultural knowledge we possess. But there is a problem. They are often stuck in **"mute."** Because they are unstructured and hard to search, these incredible moments remain locked away, underused and difficult to access.

As these collections grow, finding a specific story or a particular teaching becomes impossible without relying on someone's memory to know exactly which tape to play.

## How It Works (Without the Tech Jargon)

We have designed a system that acts like a tireless digital archivist. Here is how it helps preserve your unique voice:

### It Listens and Writes (Transcription)

The system takes spoken content and turns it into text. It is built to understand multiple languages, regional dialects, and the unique speaking styles found in faith and culture.

### It Feels the Room (Sentiment)

In faith, **how something is said matters just as much as what is said**. The system looks for emotional signals—like moments of celebration, reflection, or urgency. This helps you find the "heart" of the message, not just the words.

### It Organizes the Chaos

It breaks long recordings down into meaningful segments, so you don't have to fast-forward through an hour of video to find a five-minute clip.

## Keeping Humans at the Helm

We know that technology can sometimes miss the nuance of deep spiritual matters. That is why our solution is designed to **support human expertise, not replace it**.

> [!IMPORTANT]
> The system organizes the content, but human custodians keep the final decision-making power over how it is interpreted and published.

### You Are in Control

The system organizes the content, but human custodians keep the final decision-making power over how it is interpreted and published.

### A Safety Net

If the system is unsure about a chant, a heavy accent, or a sensitive topic, it flags it for a human to review.

### Respecting the Sacred

We treat sentiment signals as descriptive tags, not judgments, ensuring that emotionally sensitive material is never oversimplified.
